# Types of Investment Accounts Tutorial

Choosing the right investment accounts is as important as choosing the right investments. Each account type has distinct tax treatment, contribution limits, and withdrawal rules that determine its optimal role in your financial plan.

## Account Categories Overview

Investment accounts fall into three broad categories:

1. **Tax-deferred accounts** — reduce taxes now, pay later
2. **Tax-free accounts** — pay taxes now, never again
3. **Taxable accounts** — no special tax treatment, maximum flexibility

## Tax-Deferred Accounts

### Traditional 401(k) / 403(b) / 457(b)

Employer-sponsored plans with pre-tax contributions through payroll deduction.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2025 Limit** | $23,500 employee ($31,000 age 50+, $34,750 ages 60–63) |
| **Total limit (with employer)** | $70,000 |
| **Tax on contributions** | Deductible (pre-tax) |
| **Tax on growth** | Deferred |
| **Tax on withdrawals** | Ordinary income |
| **Early withdrawal penalty** | 10% before 59½ (exceptions apply) |
| **RMDs** | Yes, starting at age 73–75 |

**457(b) unique advantage**: No 10% early withdrawal penalty upon separation from service, making it ideal for early retirees in government positions.

### Traditional IRA

An individual retirement account available to anyone with earned income.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2025 Limit** | $7,000 ($8,000 age 50+) |
| **Deductibility** | Full if no workplace plan; phased out at higher incomes if covered by workplace plan |
| **Tax on growth** | Deferred |
| **Tax on withdrawals** | Ordinary income |
| **Early withdrawal penalty** | 10% before 59½ (exceptions apply) |
| **RMDs** | Yes, starting at age 73–75 |

Non-deductible contributions are allowed regardless of income, which forms the basis of the Backdoor Roth strategy.

### SEP IRA

Simplified Employee Pension for self-employed individuals and small business owners.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2025 Limit** | 25% of net self-employment income, up to $70,000 |
| **Who contributes** | Employer only (you, as business owner) |
| **Tax treatment** | Same as Traditional IRA |
| **Complexity** | Very low — easy to set up and maintain |

### SIMPLE IRA

Savings Incentive Match Plan for Employees, designed for small businesses.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2025 Employee Limit** | $16,500 ($17,500 age 50+) |
| **Employer contribution** | Mandatory match (up to 3%) or 2% non-elective |
| **Early withdrawal** | 25% penalty in first 2 years, then 10% |

## Tax-Free Accounts

### Roth 401(k) / Roth 403(b)

The Roth version of employer plans. Same contribution limits as traditional versions, but contributions are after-tax.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2025 Limit** | Same as traditional ($23,500/$31,000/$34,750) |
| **Tax on contributions** | After-tax (no deduction) |
| **Tax on qualified withdrawals** | Tax-free |
| **Income limits** | None |
| **RMDs** | None (as of 2024, per SECURE 2.0) |

**Key advantage over Roth IRA**: No income limits for contributions.

### Roth IRA

The most flexible tax-free account available to individuals.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **2025 Limit** | $7,000 ($8,000 age 50+) |
| **Income phase-out (single)** | $150,000–$165,000 |
| **Income phase-out (MFJ)** | $236,000–$246,000 |
| **Tax on contributions** | After-tax |
| **Tax on qualified withdrawals** | Tax-free |
| **RMDs** | None (during owner's lifetime) |
| **Contributions withdrawable** | Anytime, tax and penalty-free |

Roth IRAs serve multiple roles: retirement savings, emergency fund backup (contributions are always accessible), and estate planning tool (tax-free inheritance for beneficiaries within 10-year rule).

### Health Savings Account

See [Health Savings Accounts](HealthSavingsAccounts) for a complete guide. The HSA is the only account with triple tax benefits and serves as both a healthcare spending tool and a supplemental retirement account.

## Taxable Accounts

### Brokerage Account

A standard investment account with no contribution limits and no special tax treatment.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Contribution limit** | None |
| **Tax on dividends** | Annually (qualified dividends at 0/15/20%) |
| **Tax on capital gains** | When realized (long-term at 0/15/20%; short-term as ordinary income) |
| **Withdrawals** | Anytime, no penalty |
| **RMDs** | None |

**Tax-efficient strategies for taxable accounts:**
- Hold tax-efficient investments (index funds, ETFs, tax-managed funds)
- Use tax-loss harvesting to offset gains
- Hold investments long-term for preferential capital gains rates
- Harvest gains in the 0% bracket during low-income years
- Use specific lot identification for cost basis management

### 529 Education Savings Plan

Tax-free growth and withdrawals for qualified education expenses.

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Contribution limit** | State-specific (typically $300,000–$500,000 lifetime) |
| **Tax on growth** | Tax-free for education expenses |
| **Non-qualified withdrawals** | Earnings taxed + 10% penalty |
| **Rollover to Roth IRA** | Up to $35,000 lifetime (SECURE 2.0) |

## Asset Location Strategy

Different investments are more or less tax-efficient. Placing the right investments in the right account types (asset location) can meaningfully improve after-tax returns:

| Account Type | Best For | Why |
|-------------|----------|-----|
| **Tax-deferred (Traditional)** | Bonds, REITs, high-turnover funds | These generate ordinary income; deferral is most valuable |
| **Tax-free (Roth)** | Highest-growth investments | Growth is never taxed; maximize the tax-free benefit |
| **Taxable** | Index funds, tax-managed funds, individual stocks | Low turnover, qualified dividends, loss harvesting possible |

## Solo 401(k) — The Self-Employed Powerhouse

For self-employed individuals without employees (other than a spouse), the Solo 401(k) combines the best features:

- Both employee and employer contributions (up to $70,000 total)
- Roth contribution option for the employee portion
- Loan provision (borrow from your own plan)
- Backdoor Roth capability via after-tax contributions (plan dependent)

## Choosing the Right Mix

The optimal combination of accounts depends on your situation:

- **Early career, lower income**: Maximize Roth accounts (Roth IRA, Roth 401(k)) while in lower brackets
- **Peak earning years**: Maximize tax-deferred accounts to reduce current taxes; backdoor Roth for additional tax-free savings
- **Self-employed**: Solo 401(k) or SEP IRA for high contribution limits
- **HDHP eligible**: Always include HSA in the mix
- **Above all limits**: Taxable accounts with tax-efficient funds
