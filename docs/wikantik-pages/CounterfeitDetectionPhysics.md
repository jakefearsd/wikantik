# Counterfeit Detection Physics: Instrumental Analysis

Identifying high-quality counterfeits in 2026 requires moving beyond visual inspection to the fundamental physical properties of the specimen.

## 1. X-Ray Fluorescence (XRF)

XRF allows for non-destructive elemental analysis. By measuring the secondary X-rays emitted from a sample when excited by a primary X-ray source, we can determine the exact metallic composition.

*   **Purity Check:** Detecting trace amounts of tungsten in gold or zinc in silver.
*   **Surface vs. Core:** Advanced scanners can differentiate between thick plating and solid alloys.

## 2. Density Metrics (Archimedes' Principle)

Measuring the specific gravity remains the most reliable check for core displacement.

$$
\rho = \frac{m}{m - m_{water}}
$$

Counterfeits using lead or tungsten cores to match weight will inevitably deviate in volume, leading to a measurable density mismatch.

## 3. Acoustic Resonance Analysis

Every coin has a unique "signature" based on its alloy and strike pressure. Digital acoustic analysis can detect internal voids or cast structures that a human ear would miss.
