# Materials Engineering: Designing the Atomic Landscape

**Materials Engineering** is an interdisciplinary field involving the discovery and design of new materials, with an emphasis on solids. In 2026, the field has transitioned from experimental "trial-and-error" to **AI-driven Predictive Modeling**, where the properties of complex alloys and polymers are simulated at the quantum level before physical fabrication.

## 1. Computational Benchmarks: The AI Shift
Modern materials science relies on **Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (MLIPs)** to bridge the gap between quantum accuracy and classical speed.

### 1.1 The MS25 Dataset (2025)
A primary benchmark for evaluating AI models in materials engineering.
*   **Target Systems**: High-entropy alloys (HEAs), disordered oxides, and reactive surfaces.
*   **Performance Metrics**: Benchmarks model accuracy on lattice constants, volumes, and **reaction barriers**—the critical parameters for [solid-state phase transitions](CrystallizationTheory).
*   **Top Architectures**: Models like **MACE** and **NequIP** dominate the benchmark, providing DFT-level accuracy with several orders of magnitude higher efficiency.

## 2. Solid-State Phase Transitions
Understanding how materials change state is critical for [Metallurgy](Metallurgy) and [Additive Manufacturing](PhysicsEngineering).

*   **Zentropy**: A theoretical benchmark established in 2025 that integrates statistical entropy with internal energy derivatives to predict the stability of high-entropy materials.
*   **Phase-Field Modeling**: 2026 standards utilize **Second-Order Phase-Field Models** to simulate the kinetics of melting, dissolution, and grain growth with unprecedented precision.

## 3. Industrial Standards: NIST AMB2025
The **NIST Additive Manufacturing Benchmarks** provide the ground truth for solid-state transitions in 3D-printed parts.
*   **Benchmark 2025-01**: Focuses on **Nickel-based superalloy 625**, benchmarking model accuracy in predicting precipitate identification and volume fractions after complex heat treatments.

## 4. Real-World Applications
*   **Sustainable Hydrogen Storage**: Using Ab-initio CALPHAD modeling to design materials that can store hydrogen at ambient temperatures.
*   **Precision Optics**: Engineering the Zerodur® substrates used in [LISA telescopes](PhysicsEngineering) for zero thermal expansion.
*   **Industrial Catalysis**: Designing "Reactive Surfaces" that reduce the energy requirements of [Biochemical Engineering](BiochemicalEngineering) processes.
