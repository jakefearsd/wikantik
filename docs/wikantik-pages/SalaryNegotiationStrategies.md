# Salary Negotiation Strategies

Salary negotiation is a critical professional skill with compound effects; a small increase in base salary early in a career yields exponential returns over decades of raises and bonuses. Effective negotiation shifts the dynamic from a confrontation into a collaborative value-discovery process.

## 1. Information Asymmetry and Anchoring
Employers inherently possess more information about the compensation band than the candidate. 
*   **The Anchor**: The first number introduced in a negotiation sets the "anchor," pulling all subsequent discussions toward it.
*   **The Counter-Measure**: Candidates must neutralize this asymmetry via market research (Levels.fyi, industry surveys) and avoid anchoring themselves prematurely (e.g., refusing to state a "desired salary" before understanding the full scope of the role).

## 2. Total Rewards vs. Base Salary
A common failure mode is focusing exclusively on the base salary. Modern compensation is a multi-variant equation:
*   **Equity & Options**: Often the largest driver of wealth in tech and finance sectors.
*   **Sign-on Bonuses**: An effective lever when the employer has strict caps on base salary bands but possesses discretionary hiring budgets.
*   **Non-Monetary Levers**: PTO, remote work flexibility, and accelerated review cycles. 
*   *Strategy*: When an employer hits a hard ceiling on base salary, pivot the negotiation to equity or sign-on bonuses to achieve the same Total Target Cash (TTC).

## 3. Leverage and BATNA
The foundation of any negotiation is the **Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement (BATNA)**. 
*   Your leverage is strictly defined by your willingness and ability to walk away.
*   Having a competing offer is the ultimate BATNA, shifting the candidate from a position of asking for more money to a position of evaluating market-rate options.
