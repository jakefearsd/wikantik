# The Berlin Wall (1961-1989)

In the early hours of August 13, 1961, East German troops began stringing barbed wire and tearing up streets along the border between East and West Berlin. This was the beginning of the Berlin Wall, which would divide the city for 28 years.

## The "Anti-Fascist Protection Rampart"

The GDR government called the Wall an "Anti-Fascist Protection Rampart," but its true purpose was to stop East Germans from fleeing to the West. Over time, the Wall evolved from simple wire to a complex system of reinforced concrete, watchtowers, and a "death strip" of sand and alarms. At least 140 people were killed attempting to cross it.

## The Fall of the Wall

In 1989, a wave of protests across Eastern Europe and a growing exodus of East Germans through neighboring countries put immense pressure on the GDR regime. On the evening of November 9, 1989, a government official mistakenly announced that travel restrictions were being lifted immediately. Thousands of Berliners from both sides converged on the checkpoints. The guards, lacking instructions, eventually opened the gates. The Wall had fallen, signaling the end of the Cold War and the beginning of German reunification.
