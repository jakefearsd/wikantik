# Warehouse Automation

Warehouse automation covers the hardware, software, and systems that perform or assist with tasks inside a distribution or fulfilment centre — receiving, put-away, storage, picking, packing, sorting, and shipping — with reduced direct human labour.

Modern automation stacks layer multiple technologies: a **software layer** that coordinates decisions, a **mechanical layer** that moves goods, and an **intelligence layer** (AI/ML) that continuously optimises throughput and resource allocation.

| Technology | Maturity | Typical ROI Horizon |
|---|---|---|
| WMS software | Very high | 1–2 years |
| Conveyor / sorters | High | 3–5 years |
| AS/RS (unit-load) | High | 5–8 years |
| AGVs | High | 4–6 years |
| AMRs | Medium-high | 2–4 years |
| Robotic picking arms | Medium | 5–10 years |
| AI-driven slotting | Medium | 1–3 years |
